# South London Neighborhoods

## 10. Brixton (Zone 2)

**Best for**: Budget-conscious young professionals, culture seekers, music lovers

**Character**: Vibrant Afro-Caribbean culture. Excellent food markets, live music, diverse community. Gentrifying while retaining character.

| Rent | 1BR: £1,800–2,400 | 2BR: £2,400–3,200 |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|

**Pros**: Excellent food scene (Brixton Village), great nightlife, strong community, affordable, good transport
**Cons**: Higher crime historically (improving), can feel edgy at night

**Transit**: Brixton (Victoria) — direct to Oxford Circus in 15 mins
**Must-do**: Brixton Village Market, Electric Brixton, Windmill pub

---

## 11. Clapham (Zone 2-3)

**Best for**: Young professionals (especially Australians), active lifestyle, social scene

**Character**: Young professional hub. Clapham Common for outdoor activities. Busy bar/restaurant scene. Safe, social.

| Rent | 1BR: £2,000–2,600 | 2BR: £2,600–3,500 |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|

**Pros**: Clapham Common green space, active social scene, good transport, safe
**Cons**: Can feel homogeneous, weekend crowds, less culturally diverse

**Transit**: Clapham Common, Clapham North, Clapham Junction
**Must-do**: Weekend on Clapham Common, brunch Northcote Road, The Windmill pub

---

## 14. Greenwich (Zone 2-3)

**Best for**: Families, history buffs, those wanting village feel with good transport

**Character**: Historic maritime village. UNESCO World Heritage Site. Greenwich Park with London views. Village atmosphere.

| Rent | 1BR: £1,700–2,200 | 2BR: £2,200–3,000 |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|

**Pros**: Beautiful historic area, Greenwich Park (stunning views), village atmosphere, good value
**Cons**: Further from central, limited nightlife, touristy around market

**Transit**: Greenwich (DLR), Cutty Sark (DLR)
**Must-do**: Greenwich Observatory views, Maritime Museum, weekend market

---

## 16. Lewisham (Zone 2-3)

**Best for**: Budget-conscious professionals, South London access

**Character**: Diverse, up-and-coming South London hub. Ongoing regeneration. Mix of Victorian and new builds.

| Rent | 1BR: £1,600–2,000 | 2BR: £2,000–2,600 |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|

**Pros**: Very affordable, good transport (DLR, Rail), improving rapidly, near Greenwich
**Cons**: Still rough around edges, less established food/bar scene

**Transit**: Lewisham (DLR, National Rail) — direct to London Bridge
**Must-do**: Hilly Fields, Sparrow restaurant, walk to Greenwich

---

## 18. Peckham (Zone 2)

**Best for**: Young creatives, adventurous types, authentic South London

**Character**: Diverse, hip neighborhood. Rooftop bars, art galleries, African/Caribbean food. Rough edges but trendy.

| Rent | 1BR: £1,700–2,200 | 2BR: £2,200–2,900 |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|

**Pros**: Great food scene, rooftop bars, arts scene, diverse, affordable
**Cons**: Higher crime, limited tube access, can feel rough at night

**Transit**: Peckham Rye (Overground, Rail), Queens Road Peckham
**Must-do**: Frank's Cafe rooftop at sunset, Rye Lane food, Bussey Building
