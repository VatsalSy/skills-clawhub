# Central London Neighborhoods (Zone 1)

## 1. Mayfair

**Best for**: High-net-worth individuals, luxury seekers, executives

**Character**: London's most prestigious address. Georgian townhouses, private members' clubs, Michelin-starred restaurants. Old-money elegance meets international wealth.

| Rent | 1BR: £3,500–5,000+ | 2BR: £5,500–8,000+ |
|------|---------------------|---------------------|

**Pros**: Walking distance to everything, extremely safe, Hyde Park adjacent
**Cons**: Extremely expensive, limited everyday amenities, quiet at night

**Transit**: Green Park, Bond Street, Piccadilly Circus
**Must-do**: Afternoon tea at The Ritz, Burlington Arcade, The Connaught Bar

---

## 2. Soho

**Best for**: Nightlife enthusiasts, LGBTQ+ community, creatives, media professionals

**Character**: London's entertainment heart. Bars, restaurants, theatres, clubs. Vibrant LGBTQ+ scene. Busy day and night.

| Rent | 1BR: £2,500–3,500 | 2BR: £3,500–5,000 |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|

**Pros**: Unbeatable nightlife, incredible restaurants, never boring
**Cons**: Very noisy, crowded, higher petty crime, limited residential space

**Transit**: Tottenham Court Road, Leicester Square, Oxford Circus
**Must-do**: Old Compton Street, Chinatown dim sum, West End show

---

## 3. Covent Garden

**Best for**: Theatre lovers, tourists wanting central base

**Character**: Pedestrianized piazza with street performers. Very touristy but charming. Theatrical heart of London.

| Rent | 1BR: £2,700–3,800 | 2BR: £4,000–5,500 |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|

**Pros**: Extremely walkable, entertainment doorstep, great dining
**Cons**: Overwhelmingly touristy, expensive, crowded, no green space

**Transit**: Covent Garden, Leicester Square, Holborn
**Must-do**: Royal Opera House, Neal's Yard, street performers

---

## 4. Marylebone

**Best for**: Professionals, foodies, those wanting upscale village feel

**Character**: Upscale village in central London. Boutique shops, excellent restaurants. Less touristy. Sophisticated without being stuffy.

| Rent | 1BR: £2,800–4,000 | 2BR: £4,000–6,000 |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|

**Pros**: Village feel in Zone 1, excellent food scene, near Regent's Park, very safe
**Cons**: Expensive, quiet at night, limited nightlife

**Transit**: Baker Street, Bond Street, Marylebone
**Must-do**: Sunday roast at The Grazing Goat, Daunt Books, Regent's Park picnic

---

## 5. Bloomsbury

**Best for**: Students, academics, culture lovers seeking good central value

**Character**: Literary and intellectual hub. Home to British Museum, UCL. Leafy Georgian squares. Less flashy than other central areas.

| Rent | 1BR: £2,400–3,200 | 2BR: £3,200–4,500 |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|

**Pros**: Best value in central London, free museums, quiet leafy squares, academic atmosphere
**Cons**: Not as stylish, limited nightlife, can feel studenty

**Transit**: Russell Square, King's Cross St Pancras, Tottenham Court Road
**Must-do**: British Museum, Russell Square gardens, The Lamb pub
