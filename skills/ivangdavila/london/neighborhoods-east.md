# East London Neighborhoods

## 6. Shoreditch (Zone 1-2)

**Best for**: Tech workers, startup founders, creatives, young professionals

**Character**: London's hipster capital. Street art, tech startups, rooftop bars, vintage shops. "Silicon Roundabout" tech hub.

| Rent | 1BR: £2,200–3,500 | 2BR: £3,000–4,500 |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|

**Pros**: Incredible food/bar scene, street art, tech community, great nightlife
**Cons**: Can feel try-hard hipster, noisy weekends, higher crime than central

**Transit**: Shoreditch High Street (Overground), Old Street, Liverpool Street
**Must-do**: Brick Lane curry, Boxpark, street art tour, Queen of Hoxton rooftop

---

## 7. Hackney (Zone 2)

**Best for**: Artists, musicians, young creatives, those priced out of Shoreditch

**Character**: Creative hub with diverse community. Victoria Park, London Fields, Broadway Market. Authentic East London vibe.

| Rent | 1BR: £2,000–2,800 | 2BR: £2,600–3,800 |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|

**Pros**: Creative community, Broadway Market, parks, more affordable than Shoreditch
**Cons**: Higher crime in some areas, patchy transport, further from central

**Transit**: Hackney Central, Hackney Wick (Overground), Bethnal Green
**Must-do**: Broadway Market Saturday, Crate Brewery, Victoria Park picnic

---

## 15. Stratford (Zone 3)

**Best for**: Budget-conscious commuters, shoppers, Olympic Park fans

**Character**: Transformed by 2012 Olympics. Queen Elizabeth Olympic Park, Westfield. Modern, excellent transport. Still developing character.

| Rent | 1BR: £1,600–2,100 | 2BR: £2,000–2,800 |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|

**Pros**: Excellent transport (Elizabeth Line!), Westfield shopping, Olympic Park, affordable
**Cons**: Lacks historic character, can feel soulless, higher crime

**Transit**: Stratford (Central, Jubilee, Elizabeth, DLR, Overground) — one of London's best connected
**Must-do**: London Aquatics Centre, Olympic Park, Westfield

---

## 17. Walthamstow (Zone 3)

**Best for**: Young creatives priced out of Hackney, families wanting East London vibe

**Character**: Creative, community-focused. Europe's longest outdoor market. Indie cafes, craft breweries, God's Own Junkyard neon museum.

| Rent | 1BR: £1,600–2,100 | 2BR: £2,000–2,700 |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|

**Pros**: Affordable, strong creative community, Victoria Line (fast), Wetlands, market
**Cons**: Zone 3 (further out), some areas suburban, gentrifying rapidly

**Transit**: Walthamstow Central (Victoria) — 20 mins to Oxford Circus
**Must-do**: God's Own Junkyard, market day, Wild Card Brewery, Wetlands walk
