# North London Neighborhoods

## 8. Islington (Zone 2)

**Best for**: Young professionals, foodies, media workers

**Character**: Affluent but vibrant. Excellent restaurant scene along Upper Street. Mix of Georgian townhouses and modern developments.

| Rent | 1BR: £2,400–3,200 | 2BR: £3,200–4,500 |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|

**Pros**: Incredible restaurant scene, good transport, theatre/comedy, safe affluent area
**Cons**: Expensive for Zone 2, Upper Street very busy, limited green space

**Transit**: Angel (Northern), Highbury & Islington (Victoria, Overground)
**Must-do**: Dinner on Upper Street, comedy at Pleasance, Camden Passage antiques

---

## 9. Camden (Zone 2)

**Best for**: Young creatives, music lovers, alternative types, students

**Character**: Eclectic and alternative. Famous markets, live music venues, canal-side walks. Goth/punk heritage.

| Rent | 1BR: £2,200–2,900 | 2BR: £2,900–4,000 |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|

**Pros**: Amazing markets, live music scene, canal walks, great transport, unique character
**Cons**: Very touristy weekends, can be rowdy at night, some areas run-down

**Transit**: Camden Town (Northern), Chalk Farm, Kentish Town
**Must-do**: Camden Market, The Roundhouse live music, canal walk to Little Venice

## Other North London Areas (Quick Reference)

### King's Cross (Zone 1)
- Transformed area, Google HQ
- 1BR: £2,200-2,800
- Excellent transport hub
- Granary Square, Coal Drops Yard

### Hampstead (Zone 2-3)
- Affluent village, literary heritage
- 1BR: £2,000-2,700
- Hampstead Heath (amazing views)
- Very safe, family-friendly

### Finsbury Park (Zone 2)
- Budget option, improving
- 1BR: £1,700-2,200
- Good transport (Victoria, Piccadilly)
- Large park, diverse

### Highbury (Zone 2)
- Quieter than Islington, residential
- 1BR: £1,900-2,500
- Near Arsenal stadium
- Family-friendly

### Crouch End (Zone 3)
- Village feel, no tube
- 1BR: £1,700-2,200
- Creative community
- Great independent shops/cafes
