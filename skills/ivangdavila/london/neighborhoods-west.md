# West London Neighborhoods

## 12. Notting Hill (Zone 2)

**Best for**: Affluent families, professionals, those seeking picturesque London

**Character**: Pastel-colored townhouses, antique shops, Portobello Road Market. Famous from the film. Affluent, charming, village-like.

| Rent | 1BR: £2,400–3,200 | 2BR: £3,500–5,000 |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|

**Pros**: Beautiful architecture, Portobello Market, safe family-friendly, near Holland Park
**Cons**: Expensive, touristy (Portobello), can feel like a movie set

**Transit**: Notting Hill Gate (Central, Circle, District), Ladbroke Grove
**Must-do**: Portobello Road Market (Saturday), pastel houses photos, Electric Cinema

---

## 13. Fulham (Zone 2-3)

**Best for**: Young families, professionals wanting space, Chelsea-lite lifestyle

**Character**: Family-friendly with village atmosphere. Good pubs, riverside walks. More affordable alternative to Chelsea.

| Rent | 1BR: £2,200–3,000 | 2BR: £3,000–4,200 |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|

**Pros**: Family-friendly, good pubs/restaurants, riverside access, more affordable than Chelsea
**Cons**: Limited nightlife, transport not as good, can feel suburban

**Transit**: Fulham Broadway (District), Parsons Green (District), Imperial Wharf
**Must-do**: Sunday lunch at The Harwood Arms, riverside walk, North End Road Market

## Other West London Areas (Quick Reference)

### Hammersmith (Zone 2)
- Good transport hub
- 1BR: £1,900-2,400
- Near Imperial College
- Riverside pubs

### Chiswick (Zone 3)
- Village feel, family-oriented
- 1BR: £1,800-2,300
- Near Kew Gardens
- Excellent restaurants

### Shepherd's Bush (Zone 2)
- Budget-friendly west
- 1BR: £1,700-2,200
- Westfield shopping
- Near BBC studios

### Kensington (Zone 1-2)
- Upscale, museum quarter
- 1BR: £2,500-3,500
- Natural History Museum, V&A
- Safe, affluent
