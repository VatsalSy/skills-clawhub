## Booking Strategy

1. Use fewer bases and stay 2+ nights where possible.
2. Prioritize station access and neighborhood fit over pure price.
3. Book high-demand seasonal windows early.

## Tokyo

- **Best convenience:** Shinjuku, Tokyo Station, and major transit-connected zones
- **Best value compromise:** Ueno, Asakusa, and selected east-side areas
- **Watch:** Tiny-room tradeoffs in ultra-central budget listings

## Kyoto

- **Best convenience:** Central and station-adjacent zones
- **Best atmosphere:** Gion/Higashiyama edge with early-booking discipline

## Osaka

- **Best convenience:** Umeda and Namba-access corridors
- **Better nightlife value:** Tenma and nearby local neighborhoods

## Hiroshima and West Add-ons

- **Hiroshima city:** strong base for museum plus island split
- **Miyajima overnight:** good only when route pacing supports it

## Property Types

| Style | Best for | Tradeoff |
|-------|----------|----------|
| Hotel | Short city stays | Smaller rooms in prime zones |
| Ryokan | Traditional experience | Higher cost and less late flexibility |
| Apartment hotel | Families and longer stays | Quality consistency varies |
| Business hotel | Efficient logistics | Limited space and amenities |
