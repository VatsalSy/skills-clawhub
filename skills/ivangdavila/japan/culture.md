## Social Norms

- Quiet public behavior is expected in transit and shared spaces.
- Queue discipline and personal space are strongly respected.
- Polite basic greetings improve interactions quickly.

## Practical Etiquette

- Remove shoes when clearly indicated.
- Keep voice volume low on trains and in residential streets at night.
- Follow photography and privacy rules in temples, shrines, and local neighborhoods.

## Dining and Payment Notes

- Reservations matter in high-demand restaurants.
- Cashless payment is common in cities, but cash backup is still useful.
- Tipping is generally not expected in typical dining contexts.

## Respect and Awareness

- Regional manners and pace can vary across Japan.
- Temple and shrine behavior expectations should be followed closely.
- Learning a few Japanese phrases usually improves service flow.
