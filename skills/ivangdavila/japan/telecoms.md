## Main Options

- eSIM plans are practical for short stays.
- Pocket Wi-Fi can be useful for groups or multi-device travelers.
- Local SIM options work well for longer stays.

## Typical Traveler Strategy

1. Compare eSIM first for solo or short city-led trips.
2. Use pocket Wi-Fi for families or multi-device-heavy days.
3. Check regional coverage if traveling to mountains or islands.

## Practical Notes

- Urban coverage is generally strong.
- Rural and mountain routes can have weaker pockets.
- Keep offline maps for rail transfers and hiking days.

## What to Check Before Purchase

- Data cap and throttling rules
- Hotspot allowance
- Coverage in planned regions
- Pickup, return, or recharge logistics
