## Emergency Numbers

- **Police:** 110
- **Fire and Ambulance:** 119
- **General emergency support:** 112 may work via some networks, but 110/119 are primary

## Health and Medical

- Travel insurance is strongly recommended.
- Keep passport copy, medication list, and emergency contacts offline.
- Major cities have English-capable clinics, but coverage varies by time and area.

## Transport and Area Safety

- Japan is generally safe, but late-night planning still matters.
- Keep transport routes clear after nightlife, especially near last-train windows.
- In crowded zones, maintain standard pickpocket awareness.

## Weather and Outdoor Safety

- Summer heat and humidity can raise risk quickly.
- Typhoon periods can disrupt rail and flights.
- Carry water, power backup, and offline maps for outdoor days.

## When to Escalate

- Call 110 or 119 for immediate danger, injury, fire, or urgent police/medical support.
- For non-urgent issues, use local non-emergency channels and hotel assistance.
