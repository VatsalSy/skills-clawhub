# Central Milan Neighborhoods

## Centro Storico and Duomo Belt

### Who It Fits
- Short-stay visitors with high location priority.
- Professionals who value central prestige and walkability.

### Strengths
- Landmark access within minutes.
- High density of retail, dining, and services.
- Strong transit connections.

### Weaknesses
- Highest rental and hotel costs.
- Heavy tourism and weekend crowd pressure.
- Limited newer building stock.

## Brera

### Why People Choose It
- Art, design, and walkable elegance.
- Strong dining and cafe mix without full tourist overload.

### Typical Trade-Off
- Smaller apartments at premium prices.
- Parking and driving friction.

## Porta Venezia

### Best For
- People who want central access with better value than Duomo.
- International residents and social scene seekers.

### What To Watch
- Some streets are very calm, others are nightlife-heavy.
- Building quality varies from renovated to old-stock.

## Decision Rules

1. Pick Duomo belt if your schedule is landmark-heavy and budget is high.
2. Pick Brera for design, galleries, and central quality of life.
3. Pick Porta Venezia for location-value balance and social diversity.
