# Milan Food Overview

## What Defines Milan Dining

Milan combines classic Lombard dishes, national Italian excellence, and one of Europe's strongest international restaurant mixes. The city also has a unique aperitivo culture that shapes evening routines.

## Core Dining Patterns

| Pattern | Practical Meaning |
|---------|-------------------|
| Espresso culture | Fast bar service, often standing at counter |
| Aperitivo window | Social pre-dinner period with drinks and snacks |
| Late dinner shift | Many kitchens become fully active in evening |
| Reservation dependency | High-demand places require booking |

## Price Bands

| Segment | Typical Spend |
|---------|----------------|
| Fast lunch | EUR 10-18 |
| Casual dinner | EUR 20-35 |
| Mid-range dinner | EUR 35-60 |
| Fine dining | EUR 90+ |

## Best Neighborhoods by Food Goal

- Brera for polished Italian and date-night options.
- Navigli for social dining and aperitivo variety.
- Porta Romana for balanced quality and local feel.
- Isola for modern concepts and creative kitchens.
- Chinatown area for strong value and diversity.

## Service Norms

- Coperto or service charges may appear.
- Bread charge is common in many traditional places.
- Tipping exists but is modest compared to US norms.

## Booking Strategy

1. Book headline restaurants 1-3 weeks ahead.
2. Reserve weekend dinner earlier than weekday.
3. Keep one flexible backup option per neighborhood.
