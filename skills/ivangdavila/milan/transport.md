# Milan Transport Guide (2025-2026)

## Network Overview

Milan has one of Italy's best urban transport systems, with integrated metro, tram, bus, and suburban rail options.

## Core Public Transport Costs

| Item | Typical Price |
|------|----------------|
| Urban single ticket | EUR 2.20 |
| 24-hour pass | EUR 7.60 |
| 3-day pass | EUR 15.50 |
| Monthly urban pass | EUR 39 |
| Annual urban pass | EUR 330 |

## Mode Breakdown

| Mode | Best Use | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| Metro | Fast cross-city movement | Backbone for most commutes |
| Tram | Neighborhood-level movement | Great for central-medium distance |
| Bus | Coverage for gaps | Slower during rush hour |
| Suburban rail | Outer zones and commuter flow | Useful for long-distance daily routines |

## Airport Connections

| Airport | Main Option | Typical Time | Typical Cost |
|---------|-------------|--------------|--------------|
| Malpensa (MXP) | Malpensa Express train | 37-54 min | EUR 13 |
| Linate (LIN) | Metro and bus links | 20-35 min | Urban fare |
| Bergamo (BGY) | Coach and rail combos | 50-80 min | EUR 10-20 |

## Taxi and Ride-Hailing Reality

- Taxis are reliable but expensive compared to transit.
- Airport transfers can vary by route and traffic.
- For daily routines, public transport is usually more efficient.

## Strike and Disruption Planning

1. Check strike notices in advance.
2. Keep backup route options.
3. Allow buffer for airport travel on strike-risk days.

## Transport Traps

- Not validating paper tickets.
- Choosing accommodation without checking real commute time.
- Overusing taxis in peak congestion windows.
