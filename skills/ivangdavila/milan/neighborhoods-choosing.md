# How To Choose a Milan Neighborhood

## Step 1: Define Non-Negotiables

Choose top two only:
- Monthly housing budget ceiling.
- Max acceptable door-to-door commute.
- Noise tolerance at night.
- Need for green space or schools.
- Need for nightlife and restaurant density.

## Step 2: Pick Lifestyle Archetype

| Archetype | Best Starting Areas |
|-----------|---------------------|
| Central prestige | Duomo belt, Brera |
| Social and trendy | Navigli, Isola, Porta Romana |
| Business efficiency | Porta Nuova, CityLife |
| Student and value | Citta Studi, Lambrate, Bicocca |
| Family and calm | Affori, Niguarda, CityLife edges |

## Step 3: Validate Commute in Real Time

- Test commute at rush hour, not midday.
- Include walking and line change friction.
- Confirm late-evening transport frequency.

## Step 4: Check Total Cost, Not Just Rent

Include:
- Building expenses.
- Utilities and internet.
- Transit pass or parking costs.
- Event-season pricing risk for short stays.

## Step 5: Do a Street-Level Check

1. Daytime walk.
2. Nighttime walk.
3. Grocery and pharmacy distance.
4. Street noise and lighting.
5. Building entrance quality.

## Fast Decision Matrix

| Priority | Recommended Direction |
|----------|------------------------|
| Fast city access | Inner ring and high-metro zones |
| Best value | Outer ring with direct metro |
| High social life | Navigli, Isola, Porta Romana |
| Family stability | CityLife edges, Affori, Niguarda |
