# Milan Lodging Guide

## Area Comparison for Visitors

| Area | Best For | Typical Nightly Range | Trade-Off |
|------|----------|-----------------------|-----------|
| Duomo / Centro Storico | First-time visitors, landmarks | EUR 180-450 | Highest prices, crowds |
| Brera | Culture, walkability, dining | EUR 170-380 | Limited budget options |
| Porta Venezia | Good location-value balance | EUR 120-260 | Some streets noisy at night |
| Navigli | Nightlife and social atmosphere | EUR 110-260 | Late-night noise and crowds |
| Porta Romana | Food and local vibe | EUR 120-280 | Less iconic views |
| Isola / Porta Nuova | Modern style, business access | EUR 130-320 | Less "classic Milan" feel |
| Centrale Station Area | Transit convenience | EUR 90-220 | Street quality varies by block |

## Accommodation Type Guidance

| Type | Best For | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| Hotel | Short stays, reliability | Strong during business travel season |
| Aparthotel | 4-14 day trips | Better kitchen and laundry options |
| Apartment rental | Families, longer stays | Check legal registration and taxes |
| Hostel | Budget solo travelers | Good around Centrale and Porta Venezia |

## Booking Windows

- Normal periods: 4-8 weeks ahead.
- Design Week, Fashion Week, major matches: 10-16 weeks ahead.
- Peak summer weekends and holidays: 6-10 weeks ahead.

## Practical Checks Before Booking

1. Walking time to metro, not only map distance.
2. Night noise reports in reviews.
3. Air conditioning quality for summer months.
4. Elevator availability in older buildings.
5. Exact check-in cutoff times.

## If Budget Is Tight

- Stay one metro ring farther out and prioritize line access.
- Consider Sunday-to-Thursday windows.
- Book refundable options early and re-price later.
