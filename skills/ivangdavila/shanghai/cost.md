# Shanghai Cost of Living Snapshot (2026)

Shanghai can be affordable or expensive depending on housing, school, and imported lifestyle choices.
Figures below are practical ranges, not fixed prices.

## Monthly budget ranges (CNY)

| Profile | Lean | Comfortable | High spend |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Single adult | 9,000-13,000 | 15,000-26,000 | 30,000+ |
| Couple | 15,000-24,000 | 24,000-40,000 | 50,000+ |
| Family of 4 (no intl school) | 25,000-40,000 | 40,000-70,000 | 80,000+ |
| Family of 4 (with intl school) | 45,000-75,000 | 75,000-130,000 | 150,000+ |

## Typical monthly costs

| Category | Common range (CNY) | Main driver |
|---|---:|---|
| Rent (1BR central) | 7,500-16,000 | District + building age |
| Rent (2BR central) | 12,000-28,000 | Compound quality |
| Utilities + internet | 350-1,100 | Seasonal AC/heating use |
| Groceries (single) | 1,200-3,500 | Local vs imported basket |
| Dining out (single) | 1,000-4,500 | Frequency + cuisine |
| Transport | 250-2,500 | Metro-heavy vs DiDi-heavy |
| Gym/fitness | 200-1,200 | Chain vs boutique studio |

## One-off setup costs

| Item | Typical range (CNY) |
|---|---:|
| Rental deposit | 1-2 months rent |
| Agency fee | 0.35-1 month rent |
| Basic home setup | 1,500-8,000 |
| Initial health checks/docs | 500-2,000 |

## Spending patterns that change totals fast

- International schools and daycare.
- Imported groceries, wine, and western brunch habits.
- Frequent DiDi at peak hours.
- Premium compounds with club facilities.

## Quick planning rule

For relocation budgeting, target 15% contingency for the first 3 months.
In 2026, rental and service prices can move by district and demand cycle.
