# Shanghai Food Practical Guide

## Dining Hours
- Breakfast shops: often 06:30-10:00.
- Lunch peak: 11:45-13:15.
- Dinner peak: 18:00-20:00.
- Many casual places close by 21:00-22:00.

## Reservation Rules
- Book 1-3 days ahead for popular restaurants.
- Book 1 week ahead for special dates and tasting menus.
- Confirm whether your table is held if you are 15+ minutes late.
- Save the restaurant name in Chinese to help drivers find it.

## Queue Tactics
- Arrive before opening for no-booking local places.
- Use off-peak windows (17:15-17:45 or after 20:15).
- Avoid mega-mall dinner rush on Friday and Saturday.

## Payment Basics
- Mobile wallets are standard; card support is uneven.
- Carry a backup card for hotels and international chains.
- Keep small cash only for tiny neighborhood shops.
- Tipping is usually not required.

## Dietary Needs
- Vegetarian is manageable in central districts.
- Vegan is possible but needs targeted restaurant selection.
- Halal options exist, strongest in specific neighborhood pockets.
- For allergies, show a short written note in simple Chinese.

## Food Safety Habits
- Choose high-turnover shops for street snacks.
- Avoid lukewarm seafood buffets late in service.
- Drink sealed bottled water when uncertain.
- If your stomach is sensitive, start with cooked dishes only.

## Delivery Versus Dine-In
- Delivery is fast and practical for routine meals.
- Dine-in is better for soup dumplings and crispy items.
- Check distance: long routes reduce texture quality.

## Practical First Week Checklist
- Save 5 nearby restaurants across price tiers.
- Test one local breakfast shop and one reliable dinner fallback.
- Build a short list for vegetarian and late-night needs.
- Keep oral rehydration salts and basic stomach medicine at home.
