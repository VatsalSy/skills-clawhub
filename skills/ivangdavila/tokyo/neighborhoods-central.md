# Central Tokyo Neighborhoods

## Minato Ward (港区)

The most international ward in Tokyo. Home to embassies, luxury apartments, and the expat elite.

### Roppongi (六本木)

**Vibe**: International nightlife hub, corporate towers, love-it-or-hate-it energy.

**Rent (1K)**: ¥140,000-200,000

**Pros**:
- 24/7 activity, always something happening
- International restaurants and bars
- Good English support
- Tokyo Midtown and Roppongi Hills nearby

**Cons**:
- Noisy, especially weekends
- Nightlife district reputation
- Tourist scams in entertainment area
- Not family-friendly

**Best for**: Young professionals who love nightlife, tech workers at nearby offices.

**Key stations**: Roppongi (Hibiya/Oedo), Roppongi-Itchome (Namboku)

---

### Azabu (麻布) / Nishi-Azabu

**Vibe**: Quiet luxury, embassy district, old money Tokyo.

**Rent (1K)**: ¥180,000-280,000

**Pros**:
- Prestigious address
- Quiet despite central location
- International supermarkets (National Azabu)
- Excellent restaurants
- Near international schools

**Cons**:
- Very expensive
- Limited budget dining options
- Hilly terrain

**Best for**: Diplomats, corporate executives, families with international school children.

**Key stations**: Azabu-Juban (Namboku/Oedo), Hiroo (Hibiya)

---

### Hiroo (広尾)

**Vibe**: Leafy, international, upscale residential.

**Rent (1K)**: ¥160,000-220,000

**Pros**:
- Green, spacious feeling
- Strong expat community
- National Azabu supermarket
- Near Arisugawa Park
- Good clinics with English support

**Cons**:
- Only one train line (Hibiya)
- Expensive
- Limited nightlife

**Best for**: Families, long-term expats, those wanting community.

**Key stations**: Hiroo (Hibiya)

---

### Akasaka (赤坂)

**Vibe**: Business district, sophisticated, hotels.

**Rent (1K)**: ¥150,000-200,000

**Pros**:
- Central location
- Multiple train lines
- High-end dining
- Near TBS and corporate offices
- Hie Shrine for green space

**Cons**:
- Quiet on weekends (business area)
- Fewer residential amenities
- Expensive

**Best for**: Business professionals, those working nearby.

**Key stations**: Akasaka (Chiyoda), Akasaka-Mitsuke (Ginza/Marunouchi)

---

## Shibuya Ward (渋谷区)

Tokyo's trend epicenter. From the famous crossing to quiet residential pockets.

### Shibuya (渋谷) Center

**Vibe**: Iconic, crowded, youth culture, constant energy.

**Rent (1K)**: ¥130,000-180,000

**Pros**:
- Best transportation hub
- Everything accessible
- Constant entertainment
- Major shopping

**Cons**:
- Extremely crowded
- Noisy
- Limited residential quality near station
- Tourists everywhere

**Best for**: Those who want maximum convenience and don't mind chaos.

**Key stations**: Shibuya (JR/Metro/Tokyu/Keio)

---

### Ebisu (恵比寿)

**Vibe**: Sophisticated, foodie paradise, balanced lifestyle.

**Rent (1K)**: ¥140,000-200,000

**Pros**:
- Excellent restaurant scene
- Ebisu Garden Place
- Good work-life balance vibe
- Multiple train lines
- Upscale but not stuffy

**Cons**:
- Expensive
- Can feel too polished for some
- Limited budget options

**Best for**: Young professionals, food lovers, creative types.

**Key stations**: Ebisu (JR Yamanote, Hibiya)

---

### Daikanyama (代官山)

**Vibe**: Tokyo's Brooklyn, boutiques, T-Site, ultra-trendy.

**Rent (1K)**: ¥150,000-220,000

**Pros**:
- Beautiful architecture
- Independent boutiques
- Calm despite central location
- Walkable to Ebisu and Nakameguro
- T-Site bookstore culture

**Cons**:
- Very expensive
- Limited station access (Tokyu only)
- Can feel too curated

**Best for**: Creatives, designers, those valuing aesthetics.

**Key stations**: Daikanyama (Tokyu Toyoko)

---

### Harajuku / Omotesando (原宿/表参道)

**Vibe**: Youth fashion meets luxury shopping.

**Rent (1K)**: ¥140,000-200,000

**Pros**:
- Iconic location
- Near Yoyogi Park and Meiji Shrine
- Shopping variety
- Good transit

**Cons**:
- Extremely crowded on weekends
- Tourist-heavy
- Limited residential areas

**Best for**: Fashion industry, young professionals, those wanting iconic address.

**Key stations**: Harajuku (JR), Meiji-Jingumae (Chiyoda/Fukutoshin), Omotesando (Ginza/Hanzomon/Chiyoda)

---

## Shinjuku Ward (新宿区)

Tokyo's busiest station and surrounding diversity.

### Shinjuku Station Area

**Vibe**: Maximum chaos, everything accessible, overwhelming.

**Rent (1K)**: ¥120,000-160,000

**Pros**:
- Best transit access in Tokyo
- Open late (entertainment district)
- Every amenity available
- Multiple department stores

**Cons**:
- Confusing station layout
- Very crowded
- Kabukicho adjacent (red light district)
- Can feel impersonal

**Best for**: Those needing maximum transit flexibility.

**Key stations**: Shinjuku (everything)

---

### Nishi-Shinjuku (西新宿)

**Vibe**: Skyscraper district, business hotels, quiet nights.

**Rent (1K)**: ¥110,000-150,000

**Pros**:
- Walking distance to Shinjuku station
- Modern buildings
- Good parks (Central Park)
- Less crowded than east Shinjuku

**Cons**:
- Corporate feel
- Quiet on weekends
- Less character

**Best for**: Business professionals, those wanting convenience without chaos.

**Key stations**: Nishi-Shinjuku (Marunouchi), Tochomae (Oedo)

---

### Yoyogi-Uehara (代々木上原)

**Vibe**: Quiet, residential, hipster cafes.

**Rent (1K)**: ¥120,000-160,000

**Pros**:
- Peaceful despite proximity to Shinjuku
- Great cafe culture
- Near Yoyogi Park
- Odakyu line to beaches

**Cons**:
- Limited nightlife
- Hilly
- Only one major line

**Best for**: Creatives wanting peace, long-term residents.

**Key stations**: Yoyogi-Uehara (Chiyoda/Odakyu)

---

### Kagurazaka (神楽坂)

**Vibe**: Little Paris, traditional geisha district, cultural fusion.

**Rent (1K)**: ¥110,000-150,000

**Pros**:
- Unique Franco-Japanese atmosphere
- Cobblestone alleys
- Traditional restaurants
- Near Waseda University

**Cons**:
- Hilly terrain
- Limited late-night options
- Can feel isolated

**Best for**: Culture lovers, those wanting traditional Tokyo.

**Key stations**: Kagurazaka (Tozai), Iidabashi (JR/Metro)

---

## Chiyoda Ward (千代田区)

Imperial Palace, business core, quiet nights.

### Marunouchi / Tokyo Station

**Vibe**: Corporate Japan headquarters, polished, professional.

**Rent (1K)**: ¥140,000-200,000

**Pros**:
- Best transit (Tokyo Station)
- Shinkansen access
- High-end shopping (Marunouchi)
- Near Imperial Palace gardens

**Cons**:
- Dead on weekends
- Few residential options
- Expensive

**Best for**: Business travelers, finance professionals.

**Key stations**: Tokyo (everything), Otemachi (5 metro lines)

---

### Akihabara (秋葉原)

**Vibe**: Electronics, anime, otaku culture central.

**Rent (1K)**: ¥100,000-130,000

**Pros**:
- Unique atmosphere
- Tech shopping paradise
- Good transit
- Affordable for central Tokyo

**Cons**:
- Niche neighborhood
- Tourist crowds
- Limited traditional amenities

**Best for**: Tech enthusiasts, otaku culture fans.

**Key stations**: Akihabara (JR/Hibiya/Tsukuba Express)
