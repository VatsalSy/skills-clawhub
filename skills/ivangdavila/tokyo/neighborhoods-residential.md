# Residential Tokyo Neighborhoods

Areas popular with long-term residents seeking balance between convenience and livability.

## Meguro Ward (目黒区)

The sweet spot: trendy enough, calm enough, connected enough.

### Nakameguro (中目黒)

**Vibe**: Instagram-famous river, hip cafes, cherry blossoms central.

**Rent (1K)**: ¥130,000-180,000

**Pros**:
- Beautiful Meguro River (stunning during sakura)
- Excellent cafe and restaurant scene
- Fashionable but not pretentious
- Direct Hibiya line to Roppongi/Ginza
- Walkable to Daikanyama and Ebisu

**Cons**:
- Crowded during cherry blossom season
- Expensive
- Parking difficult

**Best for**: Young professionals, creatives, couples.

**Key stations**: Nakameguro (Hibiya/Tokyu Toyoko)

---

### Jiyugaoka (自由が丘)

**Vibe**: European village feel, boutiques, sweets paradise.

**Rent (1K)**: ¥110,000-150,000

**Pros**:
- Charming European-style streets
- Famous for patisseries and cafes
- Family-friendly atmosphere
- Multiple train lines
- Good schools nearby

**Cons**:
- Crowded on weekends
- Can feel suburban
- Limited nightlife

**Best for**: Families, sweet lovers, those wanting charm.

**Key stations**: Jiyugaoka (Tokyu Toyoko/Oimachi)

---

### Gakugei-Daigaku (学芸大学)

**Vibe**: Local favorite, shotengai (shopping street), authentic.

**Rent (1K)**: ¥100,000-140,000

**Pros**:
- Active shopping street
- Local restaurants
- Less touristy
- Good value for Meguro
- Community feel

**Cons**:
- Only Tokyu Toyoko line
- Less flashy than neighbors
- Limited English support

**Best for**: Long-term residents, those wanting local life.

**Key stations**: Gakugei-Daigaku (Tokyu Toyoko)

---

## Setagaya Ward (世田谷区)

Tokyo's largest ward. Green, spacious, family-oriented.

### Sangenjaya (三軒茶屋)

**Vibe**: Young, lively, izakaya paradise, student energy.

**Rent (1K)**: ¥95,000-130,000

**Pros**:
- Excellent nightlife (izakaya, bars)
- Young, energetic atmosphere
- Direct to Shibuya (5 min)
- Good value
- Carrot Tower landmark

**Cons**:
- Crowded station area
- Can be rowdy at night
- Limited green space nearby

**Best for**: Young professionals, nightlife lovers, students.

**Key stations**: Sangenjaya (Tokyu Den-en-toshi/Setagaya)

---

### Shimokitazawa (下北沢)

**Vibe**: Indie culture, vintage shops, live music, bohemian.

**Rent (1K)**: ¥95,000-130,000

**Pros**:
- Unique character (Tokyo's Camden)
- Vintage and secondhand shopping
- Live music venues
- Cafe culture
- Young, creative community

**Cons**:
- Narrow streets
- Can feel chaotic
- Tourist interest growing
- Crowded weekends

**Best for**: Creatives, musicians, vintage lovers, young people.

**Key stations**: Shimokitazawa (Odakyu/Keio Inokashira)

---

### Futako-Tamagawa (二子玉川)

**Vibe**: Upscale suburban, riverside, family paradise.

**Rent (1K)**: ¥110,000-160,000

**Pros**:
- Large shopping mall (Rise)
- Tama River access
- Parks and green space
- Family-friendly
- Modern development

**Cons**:
- Feels suburban
- Far from central Tokyo (30min to Shibuya)
- Can be boring for singles

**Best for**: Families, those wanting space, nature lovers.

**Key stations**: Futako-Tamagawa (Tokyu Den-en-toshi/Oimachi)

---

### Setagaya-Daita / Kyodo

**Vibe**: Quiet residential, local life, sleepy.

**Rent (1K)**: ¥85,000-110,000

**Pros**:
- Very affordable for the area
- Quiet, residential feel
- Near Shimokitazawa
- Good local amenities

**Cons**:
- Unremarkable
- Limited nightlife
- Can feel isolated

**Best for**: Budget-conscious, those wanting quiet life.

**Key stations**: Setagaya-Daita (Odakyu), Kyodo (Odakyu)

---

## Nakano Ward (中野区)

Otaku alternative to Akihabara, surprisingly livable.

### Nakano

**Vibe**: Broadway otaku haven, practical, unpretentious.

**Rent (1K)**: ¥85,000-120,000

**Pros**:
- Nakano Broadway (anime, manga, collectibles)
- Excellent value for central location
- 5 min to Shinjuku
- Good local dining
- Practical amenities

**Cons**:
- Less fashionable
- Niche appeal
- Station area crowded

**Best for**: Otaku, budget seekers, practical people.

**Key stations**: Nakano (JR Chuo/Tozai)

---

## Suginami Ward (杉並区)

Long residential streets, local shotengai, authentic Tokyo life.

### Koenji (高円寺)

**Vibe**: Counterculture, vintage shops, musicians, bohemian.

**Rent (1K)**: ¥80,000-110,000

**Pros**:
- Strong counterculture scene
- Excellent vintage shopping
- Live music venues
- Affordable
- Famous Awa Odori festival

**Cons**:
- Can feel rough around edges
- Limited late-night transit
- Narrow streets

**Best for**: Musicians, artists, budget creatives.

**Key stations**: Koenji (JR Chuo/Sobu)

---

### Ogikubo (荻窪)

**Vibe**: Quiet residential, ramen town, practical.

**Rent (1K)**: ¥80,000-110,000

**Pros**:
- Famous for ramen (Ogikubo Ramen)
- Multiple train lines
- Good value
- Quiet residential areas
- Practical amenities

**Cons**:
- Not exciting
- Suburban feel
- Limited nightlife

**Best for**: Ramen lovers, families, practical people.

**Key stations**: Ogikubo (JR Chuo/Marunouchi)

---

### Asagaya (阿佐ヶ谷)

**Vibe**: Artistic, quiet, jazz culture.

**Rent (1K)**: ¥80,000-110,000

**Pros**:
- Famous jazz scene
- Tanabata festival
- Quiet residential
- Good shopping street
- Affordable

**Cons**:
- Limited transit options
- Quiet (too quiet for some)
- Can feel isolated

**Best for**: Jazz lovers, writers, those wanting peace.

**Key stations**: Asagaya (JR Chuo/Sobu)

---

## Toshima Ward (豊島区)

### Ikebukuro Area

**Vibe**: Major hub, anime culture, accessible.

**Rent (1K)**: ¥95,000-130,000

**Pros**:
- Major transportation hub
- Large shopping (Sunshine City, Seibu, Tobu)
- Anime/otaku culture (Otome Road)
- Good value for hub area

**Cons**:
- Crowded
- Less fashionable than Shibuya/Shinjuku
- Can feel overwhelming

**Best for**: Commuters, anime fans, practical shoppers.

**Key stations**: Ikebukuro (JR/Metro/Tobu/Seibu)

---

### Mejiro (目白)

**Vibe**: Quiet, refined, university area.

**Rent (1K)**: ¥100,000-140,000

**Pros**:
- Peaceful despite central location
- Near Gakushuin University
- Walking distance to Ikebukuro
- Parks nearby

**Cons**:
- Quiet nightlife
- Limited dining options
- Can feel sleepy

**Best for**: Students, academics, those wanting quiet central.

**Key stations**: Mejiro (JR Yamanote)
