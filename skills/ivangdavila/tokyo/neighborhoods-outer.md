# Outer Tokyo & Beyond 23 Wards

Budget-friendly, spacious, quality of life focused.

## Nerima Ward (練馬区)

Tokyo's greenest ward. Budget paradise with decent connections.

### Nerima Station Area

**Vibe**: Quiet suburban, family-oriented, affordable.

**Rent (1K)**: ¥65,000-90,000

**Pros**:
- Very affordable
- Green spaces (Nerima parks)
- Toshimaen amusement park area
- Multiple train lines
- Safe, family-friendly

**Cons**:
- 30-40min to central Tokyo
- Not exciting
- Limited nightlife
- Suburban feel

**Best for**: Families, budget seekers, those wanting space.

**Key stations**: Nerima (Seibu/Oedo)

---

### Shakujii-Koen (石神井公園)

**Vibe**: Nature lover's haven, peaceful, family paradise.

**Rent (1K)**: ¥60,000-85,000

**Pros**:
- Large lake park (Shakujii Park)
- Very peaceful
- Extremely affordable
- Good for cyclists
- Safe area

**Cons**:
- 45min to central Tokyo
- Very quiet
- Limited amenities
- Suburban

**Best for**: Nature lovers, families, extreme budget seekers.

**Key stations**: Shakujii-Koen (Seibu Ikebukuro)

---

## Adachi Ward (足立区)

Tokyo's most affordable ward. Working class, improving reputation.

### Kitasenju (北千住)

**Vibe**: Major hub, university area, surprisingly lively.

**Rent (1K)**: ¥70,000-95,000

**Pros**:
- Major transit hub (5 lines)
- Tokyo Denki University presence
- Active shopping streets
- Izakaya culture
- Best value major hub

**Cons**:
- Historically rough reputation (improving)
- Far from west Tokyo
- Not fashionable
- Limited expat services

**Best for**: Budget seekers, students, commuters.

**Key stations**: Kitasenju (JR/Metro/Tobu/Tsukuba Express)

---

## Edogawa Ward (江戸川区)

Easternmost ward. Families, parks, affordable.

### Kasai / Nishi-Kasai

**Vibe**: Indian community hub, family parks, affordable.

**Rent (1K)**: ¥65,000-90,000

**Pros**:
- Kasai Rinkai Park (beach, aquarium)
- Large Indian community
- Very affordable
- Family-friendly
- Close to Disney area

**Cons**:
- Very far from west Tokyo
- Limited transit options
- Suburban
- Limited nightlife

**Best for**: Families, Indian expats, beach lovers, budget seekers.

**Key stations**: Kasai (Tozai), Nishi-Kasai (Tozai)

---

## Itabashi Ward (板橋区)

Northern Tokyo. Affordable, local, no-frills.

### Itabashi Area

**Vibe**: Local shopping, affordable, working class.

**Rent (1K)**: ¥65,000-90,000

**Pros**:
- Very affordable
- Active shopping streets
- Local atmosphere
- Good transit to Ikebukuro
- Practical amenities

**Cons**:
- Not exciting
- Far from fashionable areas
- Limited English support
- Industrial pockets

**Best for**: Budget seekers, long-term residents, practical people.

**Key stations**: Itabashi (JR), Itabashi-ku (Tobu)

---

## Ota Ward (大田区)

Near Haneda Airport. Industrial, diverse, improving.

### Kamata (蒲田)

**Vibe**: Working class hub, izakaya paradise, authentic.

**Rent (1K)**: ¥75,000-100,000

**Pros**:
- Great izakaya scene
- Near Haneda Airport
- Authentic local atmosphere
- Affordable
- Multiple train lines

**Cons**:
- Rough reputation
- Industrial areas nearby
- Not fashionable
- Limited expat services

**Best for**: Frequent flyers, izakaya lovers, budget seekers.

**Key stations**: Kamata (JR/Tokyu)

---

### Oimachi (大井町)

**Vibe**: Convenient, improving, practical.

**Rent (1K)**: ¥90,000-120,000

**Pros**:
- Good transit (Rinkai, Tokyu, JR)
- Near Shinagawa
- Shopping options
- Developing area
- Reasonable value

**Cons**:
- Not charming
- Industrial history
- Limited character
- Can feel boring

**Best for**: Commuters, practical people, Shinagawa workers.

**Key stations**: Oimachi (JR/Rinkai/Tokyu)

## Outside 23 Wards

### Kichijoji (吉祥寺) — Musashino City

**Vibe**: Consistently voted "most desirable place to live." Park life, hip shops, perfect balance.

**Rent (1K)**: ¥95,000-130,000

**Pros**:
- Inokashira Park (lake, boats, Ghibli museum nearby)
- Excellent shopping and dining
- Perfect work-life balance
- Creative, young atmosphere
- Harmonica Yokocho (retro alleys)

**Cons**:
- Crowded (very popular)
- Outside 23 wards (address status)
- 20min to Shinjuku
- Competition for housing

**Best for**: Quality of life seekers, creatives, families, long-term residents.

**Key stations**: Kichijoji (JR Chuo/Keio Inokashira)

---

### Mitaka (三鷹) — Mitaka City

**Vibe**: Quieter Kichijoji neighbor, Ghibli Museum, residential.

**Rent (1K)**: ¥80,000-110,000

**Pros**:
- Ghibli Museum location
- Quieter than Kichijoji
- Good value
- Near Inokashira Park
- Family-friendly

**Cons**:
- Less exciting than Kichijoji
- Longer commute
- Limited nightlife
- Suburban feel

**Best for**: Ghibli fans, families, budget seekers wanting quality area.

**Key stations**: Mitaka (JR Chuo)

---

### Tachikawa (立川) — Tachikawa City

**Vibe**: Western Tokyo hub, shopping, spacious.

**Rent (1K)**: ¥70,000-95,000

**Pros**:
- Major shopping (Lumine, Takashimaya)
- Showa Kinen Park (huge)
- IKEA, Costco accessible
- Affordable
- Good transit hub

**Cons**:
- 40min to Shinjuku
- Very suburban
- Limited nightlife
- Far from central Tokyo

**Best for**: Families, suburban lifestyle seekers, budget seekers.

**Key stations**: Tachikawa (JR Chuo/Nambu/Monorail)

---

### Chofu (調布) — Chofu City

**Vibe**: Film industry town, affordable, green.

**Rent (1K)**: ¥70,000-95,000

**Pros**:
- Film studios (Chofu is Japan's Hollywood)
- Affordable
- Near Jindai-ji Temple (nice area)
- Good parks
- Keio line direct to Shinjuku

**Cons**:
- 30min to Shinjuku
- Suburban
- Limited nightlife
- Not exciting

**Best for**: Film industry workers, families, budget seekers.

**Key stations**: Chofu (Keio)

---

### Yokohama Connection

Many Tokyo workers live in Yokohama for value and lifestyle.

**Commute**: 25-35min to Shibuya/Shinagawa via Tokyu Toyoko or JR.

**Popular areas**:
- **Yokohama Station**: Major hub, urban
- **Motomachi-Chukagai**: Chinatown, international
- **Minato Mirai**: Waterfront, modern

**Rent (1K)**: ¥70,000-110,000 (significantly cheaper than central Tokyo for similar quality)

**Best for**: Those working in south Tokyo, international atmosphere seekers, budget-conscious professionals.
