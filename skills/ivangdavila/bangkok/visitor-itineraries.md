# Bangkok Itineraries

## 1 Day: Bangkok Essentials

**Focus**: Temples, food, and contrast between old and new Bangkok

### Morning (8:00 AM - 12:00 PM)
1. **Grand Palace & Wat Phra Kaew** (8:30 AM opening)
   - Arrive before opening to beat heat and crowds
   - 2 hours exploring

2. **Wat Pho** (5-minute walk)
   - Reclining Buddha, less crowded than Grand Palace
   - Get a Thai massage at the temple school (30 min, ~300 THB)

### Lunch (12:00 PM - 1:30 PM)
**Thip Samai Pad Thai** (near Wat Saket)
- Most famous pad thai in Bangkok
- Expect a queue but it moves fast
- Order "superb" version with shrimp

### Afternoon (1:30 PM - 5:00 PM)
3. **Wat Arun** (boat from Tha Tien, 4 THB)
   - Cross the river, climb the steep prang
   - Best light for photos in afternoon

4. **Rest/Cool Down**
   - Head to a mall (Siam Paragon, IconSiam) for AC
   - Or grab a coffee at a riverside café

### Evening (5:00 PM - 10:00 PM)
5. **Rooftop Bar Sunset**
   - Sky Bar at Lebua or Vertigo at Banyan Tree
   - Dress code: no shorts/sandals
   - One drink for the view (~400-600 THB)

6. **Chinatown for Dinner** (MRT Hua Lamphong)
   - Yaowarat Road lights up after 6 PM
   - Must-try: T&K Seafood, sidewalk BBQ, mango sticky rice

## 3 Days: Deep Dive

### Day 1: Old Bangkok (Rattanakosin)
*Same as 1-day itinerary with additions*

**Add**: Khlong boat tour in afternoon (before rooftop)
- Hire longtail at Tha Tien (1,000-1,500 THB)
- See canal life, old houses, orchid farms

### Day 2: Modern Bangkok + Markets

**Morning**
- Sleep in (temples were exhausting)
- Brunch at After You Dessert Café (Siam Square) or Roast (Thonglor)

**Midday (11 AM - 3 PM)**
- **Jim Thompson House** (BTS National Stadium)
- Walk to **MBK Center** — massive local mall, cheap phones/clothes
- Or **Siam Paragon** for upscale AC and food court

**Afternoon (3 PM - 6 PM)**
- **Lumphini Park** — watch monitor lizards, escape the noise
- Coffee at **Rocket Coffeebar** (Sathorn)

**Evening**
- **Asiatique Night Market** (free boat from BTS Saphan Taksin)
- Dinner at riverside restaurants
- Optional: Muay Thai show at Asiatique

### Day 3: Sukhumvit + Weekend Options

**If Weekend (Saturday/Sunday)**
- **Chatuchak Weekend Market** (BTS Mo Chit, 9 AM - 2 PM)
- Essential: Sections 2-4 (clothes), 7 (antiques), 25-27 (art)
- Lunch: Viva 8 (section 26) or food section
- Warning: It's exhausting. Take breaks, hydrate.

**If Weekday**
- **Or Tor Kor Market** (next to Chatuchak) — best fresh market, open daily
- Breakfast/lunch here instead

**Afternoon**
- Explore **Thonglor** (BTS Thong Lo)
- Hipster cafés, galleries, design shops
- Get a foot massage (200-300 THB/hour)

**Evening**
- Dinner on **Sukhumvit Soi 38** (street food) or restaurant in Thonglor
- Drinks at **Octave Rooftop** (Marriott Sukhumvit) or **Iron Fairies** (Thonglor)
- If nightlife: **Sukhumvit Soi 11** for clubs and bars

## 7 Days: Complete Bangkok + Day Trips

### Days 1-3
Follow 3-day itinerary above

### Day 4: Ayutthaya Day Trip

**Getting There**
- Train from Hua Lamphong (6:40 AM departure, 2h, 20 THB)
- Or minivan from Victory Monument (1.5h, 70 THB)

**In Ayutthaya**
- Rent bicycle at station (50 THB/day)
- Must-see: Wat Mahathat (Buddha head in tree), Wat Phra Si Sanphet, Wat Chaiwatthanaram
- Lunch at riverside floating restaurants

**Return**: Train or van back, arrive Bangkok ~7 PM

### Day 5: Neighborhoods + Wellness Day

**Morning**
- **Ari neighborhood** (BTS Ari)
- Trendy cafés, vintage shops, local vibe
- Breakfast at Casa Lapin or Paper Butter and the Burger

**Afternoon: Spa Day**
- **Health Land** (multiple locations) — best value Thai massage
- 2-hour Thai massage: ~600 THB
- Or splurge at **Divana** or **Let's Relax** (nicer ambiance)

**Evening**
- Dinner in **Ari** area
- Rooftop at **Above Eleven** (Sukhumvit Soi 11)

### Day 6: Culture & Cooking

**Morning: Thai Cooking Class**
- Book in advance: Silom Thai Cooking School, Baipai, or Maliwan
- Usually includes market tour + 4-5 dishes
- ~1,500-2,500 THB

**Afternoon**
- **Wat Traimit** (Golden Buddha, Chinatown)
- Explore **Pahurat** (Little India) nearby
- Walk through **Flower Market** (Pak Khlong Talat)

**Evening**
- Return to Chinatown for dinner (different street than Day 1)
- Or: Train Night Market Ratchada (photogenic, colorful tents)

### Day 7: Flexible

**Options based on interests:**

**Beach escape**: Day trip to Koh Samet or Koh Sichang (2-3 hours each way)

**More temples**: Wat Benchamabophit (Marble Temple), Wat Suthat, Giant Swing area

**Shopping**: Platinum Fashion Mall (wholesale fashion), Chatuchak if weekend

**Nightlife recovery**: Sleep in, pool day at hotel, easy evening

## Itinerary Tips

### Beat the Heat
- Start early (7-8 AM for temples)
- Long AC break 12-3 PM
- Resume activities at 4-5 PM

### Transport Logic
- **Old Town days**: Chao Phraya boats + walking
- **Sukhumvit days**: BTS all the way
- **Between areas**: Grab (avoid rush hour 7-9 AM, 5-8 PM)

### Packing for the Day
- Sarong/cover-up for temples
- Sunscreen, water, fan
- Small umbrella (sun or rain)
- Hand sanitizer for street food

### Money
- 500-800 THB/day for food is comfortable
- ATMs charge 220 THB withdrawal — bring cash or fee-free card
- Haggle at markets (start at 50% of asking price)
