# Sukhumvit Neighborhoods

The main expat corridor — stretches from Phloen Chit to Bang Na along BTS Sukhumvit line.

## Asok (BTS Asok / MRT Sukhumvit)

### Overview
- **The hub**: Only station where BTS and MRT connect
- **Character**: Busy, commercial, diverse mix of everything
- **Who lives here**: First-time expats, business travelers, everyone

### Pros
- Best transit access in Bangkok
- Terminal 21 mall (quirky floors themed by country)
- Restaurants, bars, 24/7 convenience
- Walk to both business areas (Sukhumvit + Silom via MRT)

### Cons
- Crowded, noisy, hectic
- Near Nana red light area (some spillover)
- Premium prices for the location

### Key Spots
- **Terminal 21**: Weird themed mall, amazing food court (40-60 THB meals)
- **Exchange Tower**: Coworking spaces
- **Soi Cowboy**: Neon red light street (tourist attraction at this point)

### Rent
- **1BR condo**: ฿20,000-45,000/month
- **Studios**: ฿12,000-25,000/month

## Thonglor (BTS Thong Lo)

### Overview
- **The cool kid**: Bangkok's trendiest neighborhood
- **Character**: Cafés, rooftop bars, boutiques, Japanese restaurants
- **Who lives here**: Young expats, creatives, digital nomads, hipsters

### Pros
- Best café and restaurant scene
- Active social/dating scene
- Coworking spaces everywhere
- Night markets and food streets
- Walk to Ekkamai for bus station (trips outside Bangkok)

### Cons
- Expensive for what you get
- Some sois are far from BTS (need motorbike taxi)
- Trendy = can feel pretentious
- Noise on weekends

### Key Spots
- **72 Courtyard**: Design shops, cafés
- **Eight Thonglor**: Japanese dining complex
- **J Avenue**: Another mall/lifestyle center
- **Soi 38**: Night food market (best street food on Sukhumvit)
- **Iron Fairies**: Atmospheric cocktail bar
- **Roast Coffee**: Work-friendly café

### Rent
- **1BR condo**: ฿25,000-55,000/month
- **Premium buildings**: ฿40,000-80,000/month

---

## Ekkamai (BTS Ekkamai)

### Overview
- **Thonglor's quieter sibling**: Same vibe, less hype
- **Character**: Residential with pockets of trendy
- **Who lives here**: Expats who want Thonglor feel without prices

### Pros
- Eastern Bus Terminal (trips to Pattaya, islands)
- Good cafés and restaurants
- More residential, less chaotic
- Slightly cheaper than Thonglor

### Cons
- Less happening than Thonglor
- Some areas feel suburban
- Limited nightlife

### Key Spots
- **Gateway Ekkamai**: Japanese-themed mall
- **Major Cineplex**: Nice movie theater
- **Ekkamai Bus Terminal**: Buses to east coast
- **W District**: Community mall with cafés

### Rent
- **1BR condo**: ฿20,000-40,000/month

---

## Phrom Phong (BTS Phrom Phong)

### Overview
- **Japanese expat hub**: Little Tokyo of Bangkok
- **Character**: Upscale, family-friendly, excellent shopping
- **Who lives here**: Japanese families, families with kids, executives

### Pros
- EmQuartier and Emporium malls (best in Bangkok)
- Japanese supermarkets, restaurants, schools
- Benchasiri Park for kids
- Very walkable around BTS

### Cons
- Expensive rent
- Can feel sterile/mall-dependent
- Less "Thai" than other areas

### Key Spots
- **EmQuartier**: Best mall in Bangkok, rooftop garden
- **Emporium**: Classic luxury mall
- **Benchasiri Park**: Small park, events
- **Soi 24/26**: Japanese restaurants

### Rent
- **1BR condo**: ฿25,000-50,000/month
- **Family apartments**: ฿50,000-100,000/month

---

## On Nut (BTS On Nut)

### Overview
- **Best value on the BTS**: Still connected, half the rent
- **Character**: Local Thai + budget expats + digital nomads
- **Who lives here**: Teachers, budget nomads, long-term expats

### Pros
- ฿12,000-25,000 for nice 1BR condos
- Tesco Lotus, Big C, local markets
- Real Thai neighborhood feel
- 20 min to Siam by BTS
- Century Movie Plaza (cheap movies)

### Cons
- 6 stops from Siam (20 min)
- Less trendy dining/nightlife
- Some areas can flood in rainy season

### Key Spots
- **Tesco Lotus On Nut**: Huge supermarket
- **Soi 77 (On Nut)**: Food, massage, local life
- **W101**: Budget coworking
- **Sukhumvit 50**: Crossing with good restaurants

### Rent
- **1BR condo**: ฿12,000-25,000/month
- **Studios**: ฿8,000-15,000/month

---

## Phra Khanong (BTS Phra Khanong)

### Overview
- **On Nut's neighbor, even cheaper**: Up-and-coming
- **Character**: Very local, some new developments
- **Who lives here**: Teachers, very budget-conscious nomads

### Pros
- Rock-bottom rent for BTS access
- Local markets, street food
- Some new coworking spaces arriving
- Authentic neighborhood feel

### Cons
- Not much to do
- Far from central
- Can feel isolated

### Rent
- **1BR condo**: ฿10,000-20,000/month

---

## Nana (BTS Nana)

### Overview
- **Red light + Middle Eastern**: Unique mix
- **Character**: Soi 4 red light, Soi 3/11 Arab quarter, diverse food
- **Who lives here**: Budget travelers, some long-term expats

### Pros
- Cheap accommodation
- Excellent Middle Eastern food (Soi 3)
- Central location
- 24/7 vibe

### Cons
- Sleaze factor (prostitution visible)
- Noise until late
- Not family-friendly at all

### Key Spots
- **Sukhumvit Soi 11**: Best nightlife street
- **Soi 3 (Soi Arab)**: Shawarma, hookah, Middle Eastern
- **Nana Plaza**: Red light complex (tourist attraction)

### Rent
- **1BR condo**: ฿18,000-35,000/month
- **Cheap rooms**: ฿8,000-15,000/month

---

## Phloen Chit (BTS Phloen Chit)

### Overview
- **Embassy Row**: Old money Bangkok
- **Character**: Upscale, quiet, embassies, expensive hotels
- **Who lives here**: Executives, diplomats, wealthy expats

### Pros
- Prestigious address
- Central Park (small)
- Walk to Lumphini Park
- Very safe, clean

### Cons
- Expensive, boring
- Limited street food/local character
- Not much nightlife

### Key Spots
- **Central Embassy**: Luxury mall
- **Park Hyatt**: 5-star hotel
- **Embassies**: US, UK, Japan nearby

### Rent
- **1BR condo**: ฿30,000-60,000/month
- **Luxury**: ฿60,000-150,000/month

## The Pattern: Sukhumvit Sois

**Odd numbers** (north side): Generally more commercial, bars, massage parlors
**Even numbers** (south side): Generally more residential, quieter

**Lower numbers (1-21)**: More touristy, red light, hotels
**Mid numbers (21-49)**: Prime expat territory
**Higher numbers (50+)**: Local, residential, cheaper
