# Bangkok Local Services

## Phone & Internet

### SIM Cards

Get one immediately on arrival.

**Providers** (all similar quality):
- **AIS** (green): Largest, most coverage
- **True Move** (red): Good for True WiFi spots
- **DTAC** (blue): Competitive, Happy Tourist SIM

**Where to Buy**:
- Airport arrival hall (฿300-500)
- 7-Eleven (budget options ฿100-200)
- Official stores (best for long-term plans)

**Tourist SIM (7-30 days)**:
- ฿300-500
- 10-30 GB data
- Unlimited social media often included
- Some international calls

**Long-term Plans**:
- ฿300-700/month prepaid
- 20-100+ GB
- Register with passport

### Mobile Internet
- 4G/5G coverage excellent in Bangkok
- 5G expanding rapidly
- Dead zones rare in central areas

### Home Internet

**Fiber Available Almost Everywhere**

| Speed | Monthly |
|-------|---------|
| 100 Mbps | ฿400-600 |
| 300 Mbps | ฿600-800 |
| 500 Mbps | ฿700-1,000 |
| 1 Gbps | ฿1,000-1,500 |

**Providers**:
- **3BB**: Most reliable
- **True Online**: Wide coverage
- **AIS Fibre**: Good service
- **TOT**: Government option

**Setup**:
- Request through condo or call provider
- Installation 1-3 days
- May need passport copy
- No credit check for prepaid

## Banking

### Opening a Bank Account

**Can foreigners open accounts?** Yes, but documentation varies.

**Easiest Banks for Foreigners**:
1. **Bangkok Bank** — Most expat-friendly
2. **Kasikorn (KBank)** — Green bank, popular
3. **SCB** (Siam Commercial) — Good app

**Requirements (varies by branch)**:
- Passport
- Work permit OR Thai address proof
- Some branches: Letter from employer
- Some branches: Minimum deposit (฿500-10,000)

**Tips**:
- Try main branches (more experience with foreigners)
- Emquartier and Siam Paragon branches often easier
- If refused, try another branch

### Mobile Banking

Thai banking apps are excellent:
- Transfer instantly (PromptPay)
- Pay QR codes everywhere
- Bill payments
- Top up phone

**PromptPay**: Instant transfer by phone number. Ubiquitous.

### ATMs

- **Fee**: ฿220 per foreign card withdrawal
- **Max withdrawal**: Usually ฿20,000-30,000
- **Tip**: Get card with ATM fee reimbursement (Charles Schwab, Wise)

### Credit Cards

Accepted at:
- Malls, restaurants, hotels: Yes
- Street food, markets: No
- Some shops: 3% surcharge

Carry cash for daily life.

## Government Services

### Immigration

**Location**: Government Complex, Chaeng Watthana (north Bangkok)

**What You Do There**:
- Visa extensions
- 90-day reporting (long-term stayers)
- Re-entry permits

**Tips**:
- Go early (7-8 AM)
- Bring all documents + copies
- Dress appropriately
- Expect queues
- Can use agents (฿500-2,000)

### 90-Day Reporting

Required for all long-term visa holders.

**Options**:
1. **In person**: Immigration office (free)
2. **Online**: immigration.go.th (when it works)
3. **By mail**: Send forms + stamped envelope
4. **Agent**: ฿500-1,000

**Don't forget** — Fines for late reporting.
