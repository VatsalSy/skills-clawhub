# Bangkok Street Food Guide

## Street Food Basics

### How It Works
- Point at what you want or say the dish name
- Most stalls specialize in 1-3 dishes
- Eat standing, on plastic stools, or take away
- Pay cash after eating (usually)
- ฿40-80 for most dishes

### Street Food Types

| Type | What | Price |
|------|------|-------|
| **Pushcart** | Mobile vendor, simple dishes | ฿30-60 |
| **Street stall** | Semi-permanent, more options | ฿40-80 |
| **Shophouse** | Open-front shop, menu | ฿50-120 |
| **Night market stall** | Tourist-oriented | ฿60-120 |

## Must-Try Street Foods

### Pad Thai (ผัดไทย)
- **What**: Stir-fried rice noodles, egg, tofu, shrimp/chicken
- **Where**: Everywhere. Best at Thip Samai (Mahachai Road)
- **Price**: ฿40-80 (tourist areas: ฿100+)
- **Tip**: "Pad thai ho kai" wrapped in egg omelette is premium

### Som Tam (ส้มตำ)
- **What**: Green papaya salad, spicy and sour
- **Where**: Isaan (northeastern) stalls everywhere
- **Price**: ฿40-60
- **Warning**: Thai-spicy is VERY spicy. Say "mai phet" for mild.
- **Variations**: Som tam thai (peanuts), som tam poo (crab — risky for stomach)

### Khao Man Gai (ข้าวมันไก่)
- **What**: Hainanese chicken rice — poached chicken, rice cooked in broth
- **Where**: Specialized stalls (look for whole chickens hanging)
- **Price**: ฿40-60
- **Best**: Pratunam Chicken (Soi 30, Pratunam)

### Pad Krapao (ผัดกระเพรา)
- **What**: Holy basil stir-fry with minced pork/chicken + fried egg
- **Where**: Everywhere — Thailand's #1 comfort food
- **Price**: ฿45-70
- **Order**: "Pad krapao moo sap kai dao" (minced pork, fried egg)

### Khao Pad (ข้าวผัด)
- **What**: Thai fried rice
- **Where**: Everywhere
- **Price**: ฿40-60
- **Variations**: Chicken, pork, shrimp, crab

### Moo Ping (หมูปิ้ง)
- **What**: Grilled pork skewers, sweet and savory
- **Where**: Morning stalls, markets
- **Price**: ฿10-15 per stick
- **Pair with**: Sticky rice (khao niao)

### Satay (สะเต๊ะ)
- **What**: Grilled marinated meat skewers with peanut sauce
- **Where**: Markets, some street corners
- **Price**: ฿10-15 per stick
- **Best**: Pork or chicken

### Gai/Moo Tod (ไก่ทอด/หมูทอด)
- **What**: Thai fried chicken or pork
- **Where**: Specialized stalls
- **Price**: ฿40-80
- **Note**: Crispier and more flavorful than Western fried chicken

### Kuay Teow (ก๋วยเตี๋ยว)
- **What**: Noodle soup — Thailand's everyday meal
- **Where**: Everywhere
- **Price**: ฿40-60
- **Customize**: Noodle type, meat, soup style

### Boat Noodles (ก๋วยเตี๋ยวเรือ)
- **What**: Intense, dark broth noodles (traditionally with blood)
- **Where**: Victory Monument (many stalls), specialized shops
- **Price**: ฿15-25 per small bowl (eat 5-10 bowls)
- **Experience**: Stack your bowls to count

## Street Food by Time

### Morning (6-10 AM)
- **Jok**: Rice porridge with pork/egg
- **Pa-tong-go**: Fried dough sticks + coffee
- **Moo ping + sticky rice**: Grilled pork breakfast
- **Khao tom**: Rice soup

### Lunch (11 AM - 2 PM)
- **Pad krapao over rice**: The Thai lunch
- **Khao man gai**: Chicken rice
- **Khao pad**: Fried rice
- **Noodle soups**: Everywhere

### Afternoon Snack (2-5 PM)
- **Mango sticky rice**: Peak season March-May
- **Roti**: Thai-Muslim pancake
- **Fresh fruit**: Cut fruit carts
- **Thai tea/coffee**: Iced, sweet, strong

### Dinner (6-10 PM)
- Night markets come alive
- Same dishes as lunch but more variety
- Grilled seafood stalls appear

### Late Night (10 PM+)
- Chinatown keeps going
- Pratunam 24/7 stalls
- Post-drinking khao tom (rice soup)

## Best Street Food Areas

### Chinatown (Yaowarat)
- **When**: Evening (6 PM+)
- **Best for**: Seafood, Chinese-Thai fusion
- **Must-try**: T&K Seafood, Soi Texas stalls

### Silom Soi 20
- **When**: Lunch only (11 AM - 2 PM)
- **Best for**: Office worker lunch rush
- **Vibe**: Authentic, fast, cheap

### Sukhumvit Soi 38
- **When**: Evening (6 PM - midnight)
- **Best for**: Famous street food strip
- **Note**: Slightly touristy but quality maintained

### Victory Monument
- **When**: All day
- **Best for**: Boat noodles, minivan food
- **Vibe**: Chaotic, authentic

### Or Tor Kor Market
- **When**: Morning-afternoon
- **Best for**: Premium ingredients, prepared foods
- **Vibe**: Upscale market, best quality

### Pratunam
- **When**: 24/7
- **Best for**: Late night, cheap eats
- **Must-try**: Pratunam chicken rice (Soi 30)

## Pro Tips

1. **Follow office workers** at lunch — they know the best spots
2. **Look for queues** — Thais wait for good food
3. **Check for "Shell" logo** — Michelin Guide's street food recommendations
4. **Avoid empty stalls** — No turnover = sitting food
5. **Condiment station**: Season your own — fish sauce, sugar, chili, vinegar
6. **Tissue fee**: Some places charge ฿2-5 for tissues (that's normal)
